The Econometric Engine
Four estimators. One platform.
Every standard panel estimator — fully implemented from first principles, with formal diagnostic suites
FE · Within Estimator
Fixed Effects
Two-way entity & time demeaning eliminates unobserved heterogeneity. F-stat tests joint significance of entity effects.
RE · GLS Estimator
Random Effects
Swamy-Arora variance decomposition. Theta-weighted quasi-demeaning. Full σ²ᵤ, σ²ₑ component output and GLS VCov.
FD · First Difference
First Difference
Removes time-invariant confounders by differencing consecutive periods. Optimal for non-stationary panels.
OLS · Pooled Baseline
Pooled OLS
Standard least squares baseline with full diagnostics — R², F-statistic, AIC, BIC, and robust SE options.
Hausman Testχ² test formally selects FE vs RE by testing endogeneity of entity effects
Breusch-PaganLM test for heteroskedasticity — flags non-constant variance needing robust SEs
Durbin-WatsonAutocorrelation detection — warns when serial correlation inflates t-statistics
Jarque-BeraNormality test on residuals — validates distributional assumptions for inference

GPT-4 · AI INTERPRETATION · FIXED EFFECTS MODEL
Model Choice: The Hausman test (χ²=12.44, p=0.014) rejects the null — confirming endogeneity of entity effects. Fixed Effects is the correct estimator.

Key Findings: tech_spend is the dominant driver — a one-unit increase yields a 1.20-unit rise in log-revenue (p<0.001), consistent with strong returns to R&D. capital shows significant positive returns (β=0.812), while labour shows a negative conditional effect (β=−0.403), possibly reflecting automation substitution.

Caveats: Breusch-Pagan does not reject homoskedasticity — standard errors are reliable. Durbin-Watson (1.84) is within the acceptable range.
